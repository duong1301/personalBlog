Yellow cake with chocolate frosting

This fluffy vanilla cake recipe is the absolute best yellow cake from scratch and paired with the silky smooth chocolate frosting, you can't beat it for a classic birthday cake or any other celebration

Are you a box mix yellow cake person? Or a from-scratch yellow cake person?

Truth be told, it's REALLY hard to replicate that always-moist-and-fluffy texture that everyone loves about a box cake mix, but at the same time, we also love homemade, right?!

It took me a LONG time to find a homemade yellow cake that really, really love. Box cake mixes seem to always bake up perfectly fluffy and moist yellow cake, and the ones I've had from bakeries have been great as well, but I just could't seem to replicate it at home no matter how many recipes I tried(Consequently, I havent't had the same issue with chocolate cake! See: Snickers Cake - the base recipe for all of my chocolate cake!)

Many were too dense, too dry, too chiffon-like, too fussy in their preparetion. I just wanted a fantastic yellow cake recipe I could use for birthday cakes that was easy to make and turned out delicious. I came across this recipe from Joy while I was on the hunt for yet another recipe to test out for Joseph's birthday.

This was IT. The recipe that had eluded me for years!

I love that this can easily be mixed together in one bowl using my hand mixer, there's nothing fussy about the ingredients or preparation, and it product an absolutely perfect yellow cake. The layer are light but sturdy, buttery and moist, and the ideal base for your very fovorite chocolate frosting.

I used the chocolate frosting recipe from my ultimate chocolate cupcakes, and it was wonderful companion to this cake. the frosting is more buttery than sugary, satiny smooth and full of rich chocolate flavor. It glides on like dream!

Yellow cake tends to be the elusive unicorn of homemade baking; so many cake can turn out too dry, too crumbly, too dense, too cornbread-like, and I have one major of advice fo ensuring yellow cake perfection:

Make sure all of the ingredients listed "at room temperature" ARE ACTUALLY AT ROOM TEMPERATURE.

I've totally been there and have used still-cool butter, eggs or dairy in a recipe when I'm in a hurry, but it's actually vitally important here. Having all of those ingredients at room temperature ensures that they emulsify, which traps air in the batter ans, once in the oven, that air exphands and produces a fluffy cake.

Room temperature ingredients also ensure that the batter comes together well and is ultra-smooth, which keeps the resulting cake from being crumbly or cornbread-like.
